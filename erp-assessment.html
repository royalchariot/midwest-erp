ERP Assessment

ERP Assessment for Businesses That Need Clarity Before Expensive Decisions

Meesam ERP reviews your current systems, processes, data, integrations, and reporting challenges to help you understand what is working, what is broken, and what should happen next.

Schedule ERP Assessment

Who this is for

Use assessment when the problem is real but the path is unclear.

Outgrowing QuickBooks

Finance and operations need more structure than spreadsheets and entry-level accounting can support.

Current ERP is underused

The system exists, but teams still work around it because workflows, reports, or permissions are weak.

Reports are unreliable

Leadership cannot trust numbers across ERP, CRM, billing, procurement, and reporting tools.

Close is manual

Month-end close depends on offline reconciliations, manual entries, and delayed approvals.

Leadership wants a roadmap

You need a clear recommendation before funding software, implementation, or optimization work.

Replace or optimize?

Assessment clarifies whether to improve the current system or prepare for ERP selection.

Deliverables

What you get.

The assessment produces a practical roadmap, not a vague recommendation to buy software.

Outcome

Know the right next move.

You will know whether to improve your current system, prepare for ERP selection, delay implementation until data and process issues are fixed, or move forward with a full ERP roadmap.

How long does an ERP assessment take?

Most assessments are structured over a few weeks depending on process complexity, team availability, and system access.

Do we need to know which ERP we want?

No. The assessment helps clarify whether selection is the right next step.

Can you review NetSuite or Dynamics?

Yes. We can assess existing NetSuite, Dynamics, Sage Intacct, Acumatica, Odoo, and connected business systems.

Will you talk to finance and operations teams?

Yes. ERP problems usually cross departments, so stakeholder interviews are part of the process.

Do you provide a written roadmap?

Yes. The deliverable is a written summary with findings, risks, and recommended next steps.

Can this lead into implementation support?

Yes. Assessment can lead into selection, implementation advisory, data migration, integrations, or optimization work.
